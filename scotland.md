8. Scotland policy

Scotland's digital and connectivity landscape spans Scottish Government investment, UK-wide schemes delivered in Scotland, and local authority services. Figures and timelines here move fast, so we link to the live official page rather than quoting numbers that will soon date.

Public investment should mean public clarity.

The Scottish Government's broadband and connectivity policy hub sets out programme descriptions in the government's own words. R100 is the flagship extension programme; check the official publications for current timelines. The refreshed Digital Strategy frames how digital tools, data, and public services develop together, with a 2025–2028 delivery plan. COSLA's Digital Office supports council collaboration on digital priorities. UK-wide frameworks (Project Gigabit, Ofcom reporting) matter too, since households experience outcomes as one lived reality regardless of which government is responsible.

Glasgow's Digital Housing Strategy 2022–2028 is the only local authority strategy to address digital housing and digital exclusion together. Its own evidence found 65% of households in Glasgow's social rented housing don't use home broadband, with 32 RSLs backing the strategy across 75% of the city's social housing stock. It names affordability, digital skills, and reluctance to transact online as the three primary barriers, and requires that traditional (face-to-face, phone) service methods continue alongside digital ones. No standalone evaluation has been published yet, so the accountability picture is still incomplete.

How to use this page as an activist: pair official programme links with local stories from your community council or tenants' union; ask how voucher pathways are advertised offline, not just online; request updates in accessible formats.

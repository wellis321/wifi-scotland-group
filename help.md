7. Help getting online

These are real schemes that can help people in Scotland get connected or pay less. Always check the official source for current eligibility and amounts, as these schemes change.

No fixed address? Read this first. Most schemes require a permanent address and contract — not available to people who are homeless or in temporary accommodation. Two exceptions: the National Databank (free SIM cards, no address required, via foodbanks/hostels/libraries) and Jobcentre Plus branches (free computers and Wi-Fi, no appointment needed).

National Databank — free SIM cards (Vodafone 40GB, O2 25GB, Three 24GB) via 4,000+ partner hubs. Ask at a local library or foodbank.

Social tariffs — cheaper broadband (£12.50–£20/month) for people on Universal Credit and other qualifying benefits. Only 1 in 12 eligible households use one, mostly because 7 in 10 have never heard they exist. No paper proof needed — just ask your provider.

R100 — Scottish Government's £697m programme extending superfast broadband to every premises; 100,000+ connected so far, completion expected 2028.

UK Gigabit Broadband Voucher Scheme — vouchers up to £3,500 for rural homes/businesses not scheduled for commercial gigabit upgrade.

Connecting Scotland — devices and connections for digitally excluded people; currently being redesigned, new applications paused.
